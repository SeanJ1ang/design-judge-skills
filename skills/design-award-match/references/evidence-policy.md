# Evidence and Source Policy

## Source precedence

Use the most specific current official source available:

1. current official competition rules, terms, or entry guide;
2. current official award, track, or category page;
3. current official judging-criteria page;
4. official FAQ or organizer announcement;
5. official winner project page for past-winner observations;
6. archived official page, clearly dated;
7. reputable third-party page for discovery only.

Never use a search-result snippet as final evidence. When sources conflict, current official rules control. State unresolved conflicts.

## Project evidence levels

- `Supplied fact`: explicitly stated by the user or shown in a supplied document.
- `Observed`: directly visible in supplied project material.
- `Inference`: reasoned from supplied material; must be labeled.
- `Unknown`: unavailable; must not be filled by assumption.

Map judging criteria only to supplied facts, observations, or clearly labeled inferences.

## Trend evidence

Use official winner pages and minimal factual metadata. Do not reproduce official images or descriptions. A defensible trend note includes:

- exact award and category;
- sample size and years;
- observable repeated characteristic;
- counterexamples or limitations;
- direct links to the sampled official pages when practical.

If the sample mixes categories, years with materially different rules, or fewer than three winners, do not generalize it as a trend.

## Freshness rule

Award rules, categories, fees, deadlines, and judging criteria are time-sensitive. Verify them at request time. If verification is impossible, label the item `Needs current official verification` and prevent it from supporting an `Eligible` gate or `High` confidence.
