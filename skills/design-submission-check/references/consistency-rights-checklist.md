# Consistency, Evidence, and Rights Checklist

## Canonical fact sheet

Create one canonical value for each material fact before comparing files:

- official project title and short title;
- applicant, team members, roles, organizations, and credits;
- award, track, category, cycle, and entry ID;
- project status, completion date, launch date, and current version;
- target user, problem, primary function, use context, and workflow;
- technology, material, dimensions, performance, sustainability, and impact data;
- research sample, baseline, denominator, unit, method, and citation;
- third-party assets, licenses, consent, and disclosure status.

Mark each canonical value `Supplied`, `Documented`, `Inferred`, or `Unknown`.

## Cross-material checks

Compare the canonical sheet with form fields, PDFs, boards, captions, diagrams, video titles, subtitles, voice-over, credits, filenames, and supporting evidence.

Flag:

- different values for the same fact;
- different project or team versions;
- claims appearing in one material but unsupported elsewhere;
- charts whose labels, units, baselines, or denominators are absent;
- images or prototypes that imply features not described as conceptual;
- inconsistent category or applicant names;
- stale filenames, duplicated finals, missing captions, or broken references.

Minor wording variation is not inconsistency when the factual meaning is unchanged.

## Claim integrity

For every material claim, record:

| Claim | Location | Evidence supplied | Scope matches evidence? | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Give special attention to `first`, `best`, `proven`, `zero`, `100%`, medical, safety, environmental, accessibility, and impact claims. Do not independently certify these claims. Ask for authoritative evidence, narrow the wording, or label the limitation.

## Rights and permission inventory

Inventory these asset types:

- photographs, renderings, illustrations, icons, maps, and stock media;
- video footage, music, sound effects, voice, subtitles, and performances;
- fonts, templates, software, code, datasets, research, and charts;
- trademarks, logos, product imagery, partner names, and client material;
- identifiable people, personal data, user research, and testimonials;
- AI-generated or AI-assisted text, images, audio, video, or code;
- confidential, embargoed, patent-sensitive, or contract-restricted information.

For each item record creator/source, owner, license or consent basis, permitted submission/publicity scope, attribution requirement, proof location, disclosure requirement, and status.

## Risk rules

- Appearance alone never proves ownership or permission.
- A web-accessible asset is not automatically reusable.
- A stock or font license may treat award submission and organizer publicity differently; verify the actual terms.
- Consent for project research may not include public award-gallery publication.
- Trademark mention and logo reproduction are separate facts.
- AI disclosure requirements are award- and cycle-specific; verify current official wording.
- `Pending`, `Unknown`, and `Restricted` must remain visible in the final report.

This checklist supports risk identification, not legal clearance.
